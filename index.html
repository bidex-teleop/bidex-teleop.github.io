Bimanual Dexterity for Complex Tasks

Abstract

To train generalist robot policies, machine learning methods often require an abundance of expert human data. A natural robot for humans to teleoperate is one that mimics themselves: bimanual arms and dexterous hands. However, building a bimanual teleoperation system for over 50DOF that accurately tracks both arms and hands is an extremely difficult problem. To address this, we introduce BiDex, an extremely dexterous, low-cost, low-latency and portable bimanual dexterous teleoperation system which relies on motion capture gloves and lightweight teacher arms. Our system is easy to use and can complete a variety of dexterous tasks in a natural intuitive manner, and enables collecting data training policies using imitation. We compare to two baseline approaches for data collection, using the Vision Pro teleoperation system and the SteamVR system. We also show BiDex operating a mobile bimanual robot for in the wild tasks. Please refer to https://bidex-teleop.github.io for more video results.
